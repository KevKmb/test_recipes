Curry

Description

An simple curry that is cooked in about 30 mins and easy to customize

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of grated fresh ginger
  • 2 tablespoons of curry powder
  • 1 medium sweet potato
  • 200 g cauliflower florets
  • 200 g broccoli florets
  • 2 medium sized carrots
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 450 g can of lentils or chickpeas
  • 2 400 mL cans of coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons thai red curry paste
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt, or more to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon sugar or maple syrup
  • 1 lime, juice squeezed
  • 300 g fresh baby spinach

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
    Now add the garlic and ginger, cook for 1 minute. Add the curry powder and
    sauté 1-2 minutes more, until fragrant
  2. To the pot, add sweet potato, cauliflower, broccoli, carrots, red pepper and lentils or
    chickpeas. Pour the coconut milk, red curry paste and salt and stir well. Bring to a boil,
    then simmer for about 10 minutes, until the sweet potatoes and the other vegetables are
    tender
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch with 2 tablespoons water, then stir into the
    curry to thicken.
  4. Now stir in the sugar, lime juice and the spinach until it wilts. Taste; season with more salt
    if desired. Serve hot with rice, fresh cilantro, hot sauce and maybe Naan
